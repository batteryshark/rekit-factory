## Goal
Persist canonical v1 trial records locally so exact retries are idempotent, conflicting trial-ID reuse fails closed, and committed records survive restart and interrupted writes unchanged.

## Requirements
- [ ] Provide append, read, and deterministic list APIs over canonical TrialRecordV1 bytes
- [ ] Publish atomically and durably without exposing a partial canonical record as committed
- [ ] Treat an exact same-ID/same-bytes retry as success and any same-ID/different-content retry as a conflict that preserves the first record
- [ ] Enforce the same invariant across concurrent writers and process restart
- [ ] Verify record identity, canonical bytes, safe storage identity, and content digest on every read/list operation
- [ ] Fail closed on tampered, truncated, noncanonical, mismatched, or symlinked records without silently replacing a committed record

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Exact retry before and after reopening is idempotent; conflicting reuse leaves the original byte-identical
- [ ] Failure before publication leaves no readable trial; failure after publication cannot roll back or replace it
- [ ] Concurrent exact writers converge and concurrent conflicting writers produce one valid winner plus one closed conflict
- [ ] Multiple completed and interrupted trials survive restart and list in deterministic order
- [ ] Tampered, truncated, noncanonical, mismatched-identity, and symlink cases are rejected

## Plan
Implement storage and focused temporary-directory tests in an isolated ReverseBench worktree after one worker slot opens. Shared exports/default paths remain an integration-owner decision.

## Notes
This is local durable record persistence only: no scheduler orchestration, Factory journal access, mutable lifecycle projection, distributed database, remote/object storage, retention, encryption, or multi-host consensus.
