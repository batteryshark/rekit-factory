## Goal
Use owned, ground-truthed reverse-engineering fixtures as a safe calibration arena for comparing models, harnesses, strategies, tools, and prospective techniques before trusting them on novel research.

## Requirements
- [ ] Integrate with ReverseBench as the owner of tasks, ground truth, scoring, and run records; Factory remains the execution/control plane
- [ ] Require each corpus item to declare ownership/license/authorization, target hash/build recipe, expected behavior/findings, allowed actions/network, reset procedure, difficulty/domain, and scoring version
- [ ] Support native, managed, mobile, web/service, packed/obfuscated, dynamic, and GUI-oriented fixture classes incrementally without requiring all classes in the first slice
- [ ] Separate development/calibration fixtures from held-out evaluation fixtures and prevent worker access to source, ground truth, private tests, public writeups, or prior solution dossiers during evaluation
- [ ] Record solve/analysis quality, false positives, independent reproduction, evidence completeness, intervention count, time, tokens/cost, tool usage, retries, crashes, and scope-policy violations
- [ ] Make model+harness+strategy+tool-profile+environment versions part of the comparison key
- [ ] Reset environments and target state between runs; isolate parallel evaluations and make non-determinism visible through repeated trials
- [ ] Import historical CTF/wargame exercises only when offline reproduction and use rights are explicit; do not automate live enrollment or submissions
- [ ] Provide baselines and regression thresholds without optimizing solely for flags or memorized final answers
- [ ] Emit machine-readable results that can gate capability promotion and release decisions

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] At least three owned fixtures with known ground truth exercise different analysis behaviors and produce objectively scored dossiers
- [ ] A source/ground-truth leakage test proves evaluated workers cannot read hidden answers through the workspace, retrieval, logs, or artifact store
- [ ] Repeating a fixed profile reports variance rather than overwriting one canonical score
- [ ] A deliberately hallucinated finding is penalized and a reproducible negative conclusion can receive credit
- [ ] Comparison output identifies profile/environment versions and remains reproducible after restart
- [ ] No calibration run contacts a public challenge or third-party target

## Plan
Agree the Factory↔ReverseBench run/result contract, add a minimal owned corpus and sealed evaluator, then comparison views and repeated-trial/regression support. Expand domains only after leakage and reset controls pass.

## Notes
CTFs are useful as controlled exercises, not as the product and not automatically as training data. Ground-truthed owned builds are preferable because they let Factory measure defensible understanding instead of merely accepting a flag.
